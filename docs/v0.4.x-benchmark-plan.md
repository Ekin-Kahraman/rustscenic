# v0.4.x post-release benchmark plan

Drafted 2026-05-05, against the v0.4.0 tag (sha 9ee6739).

## Why this exists

v0.4.0 is "publishable end-to-end" on **two public multiome datasets** —
PBMC 3k (human, adult immune) and mouse brain E18 5k (mouse, embryonic
CNS). That demonstrates the orchestrator works across species, tissue,
and developmental stage on real data; it does **not** demonstrate broad
biological generalisation.

The v0.4.x point releases close that gap by running the same
`pipeline.run(...)` orchestrator across a structured sweep of public
datasets, with the same evidence schema used for v0.3.9 / v0.3.10
artefacts (`validation/multiome_pipeline_run_*.json`):

```
release, smoke_type, rustscenic_version, rustscenic_sha,
install_command, api_call, dataset (incl. MD5 of input files),
shapes, wall_s, peak_rss_gb, outputs_non_empty, headline_counts,
biological_sanity, output_inventory, elapsed_per_stage, env, scope_notes
```

Every benchmark entry below resolves to one such JSON artefact under
`validation/v0.4.x_sweep/`.

## Dataset shortlist (6 datasets)

The shortlist optimises for: (i) public availability, no DUA;
(ii) well-characterised canonical TFs for biological sanity; (iii)
diversity across species, tissue, and scale; (iv) at least one
dataset > 50k cells to stress the orchestrator beyond current proofs.

| # | Dataset | Accession / source | Species | Tissue / state | Scale (cells) | Modality | Canonical TFs (sanity) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | PBMC granulocyte-sorted 10k | 10x Genomics public datasets, `pbmc_granulocyte_sorted_10k` (multiome) | Human | Adult immune (PBMC) | ~10,000 | scRNA + scATAC paired | SPI1, PAX5, GATA3, TBX21, IRF4, EBF1 |
| 2 | Mouse brain fresh 10k | 10x Genomics public datasets, `e18_mouse_brain_fresh_10k` (multiome) | Mouse | E18 brain | ~10,000 | scRNA + scATAC paired | Pax6, Sox2, Neurod2, Tbr1, Ascl1, Foxg1 |
| 3 | NeurIPS 2021 Multiome (Open Problems) | GEO `GSE194122` (Luecken et al. 2021) | Human | Bone marrow (CD34+ enriched) | ~70,000 | scRNA + scATAC paired (multi-site) | GATA1, KLF1, RUNX1, EBF1, SPI1, CEBPA |
| 4 | Mouse heart E18 | 10x Genomics public demo or ENCODE-DCC paired multiome (verify exact accession before kickoff) | Mouse | E18 heart | ~5,000–10,000 | scRNA + scATAC paired | Gata4, Mef2c, Nkx2-5, Tbx5, Hand2 |
| 5 | Human tonsil multiome | 10x Genomics public demo `tonsil_10k` (or HCA tonsil cell atlas if 10x demo is removed) | Human | Adult tonsil (secondary lymphoid) | ~10,000 | scRNA + scATAC paired | BCL6, IRF4, PAX5, BACH2, IRF8 |

Datasets 1, 2, 3 are confirmed-available canonical references; 4, 5, 6
are tissue-diversity expansions whose exact accession should be locked
during plan kickoff (some 10x demos rotate URLs).

Two datasets (3 and any one of 4–6) cover the >50k-cell stress test;
the rest cover tissue diversity.

## Per-dataset success criteria

A dataset run is "passing" only when **all** of the following hold,
mirroring the v0.3.9 / v0.3.10 artefact contract:

1. **Install path**: `pip install "rustscenic[validation] @ git+https://github.com/Ekin-Kahraman/rustscenic@v0.4.x"` succeeds in a fresh venv. Recorded under `install_command`.
2. **Single-call orchestrator**: a single `rustscenic.pipeline.run(rna=..., adata_atac=..., ...)` call (with documented caller-side ATAC pre-subset) completes without errors. Recorded under `api_call`.
3. **All six SCENIC+ stages emit non-empty artefacts**: GRN, AUCell, topics, cistarget, enhancer-link, eRegulon. Recorded under `outputs_non_empty` and `output_inventory`.
4. **Biological sanity**: ≥ N/N expected canonical TFs from the table above are recovered as regulons (`biological_sanity` field). N is dataset-specific (5–9 TFs depending on tissue).
5. **Determinism**: rerunning with the same seed produces a bit-identical GRN edge table (or a recorded diff explaining any departure). Recorded under `scope_notes`.
6. **Resource bounds**: peak RSS recorded; wall time per stage recorded under `elapsed_per_stage`.

The biology check is the load-bearing one. A run that finishes but
recovers 1/9 expected TFs is a regression, not a pass.

## Aggregate publication-readiness gate (v0.4.x → v0.5)

The benchmark sweep is "publication-ready" when:

- ≥ 5 of 6 datasets pass the per-dataset success criteria above.
- ≥ 2 of 6 datasets have > 50k cells.
- ≥ 4 of 6 datasets are runs that the user (or a collaborator) executed
  themselves on their own hardware, not lifted from prior logs.
- Cumulative biology recovery is ≥ 80 % across all expected canonical
  TFs across all passing datasets.
- No silent-failure paths: every "missing TF" is explicitly traced
  (e.g., "TBX21 absent because dataset is CD4+-enriched, not whole
  PBMC") rather than left unexplained.

A dataset that fails (e.g., orchestrator stalls, biology not
recovered) becomes a tracked issue, not a hidden caveat.

## Execution order

In ascending difficulty / scale:

1. **PBMC 10k** — direct scale-up of the v0.3.9 artefact. Lowest-risk
   first run; same tissue, same TFs, 3× scale.
2. **Mouse brain 10k** — direct scale-up of the v0.3.10 artefact.
   Validates the species + tissue cross-cut at 2× scale.
3. **NeurIPS BMMC 70k** — first > 50k-cell stress test. Bone marrow is
   different from PBMC despite both being immune. Multi-site
   donor-effect data tests batch robustness implicitly.
4. **Mouse heart E18** — first non-immune, non-CNS organ. Lock the
   accession before starting.
5. **Human tonsil** — secondary lymphoid; complementary immune biology
   to PBMC.
6. **Breast cancer multiome** — disease-state, not healthy.

Datasets 1 + 2 are the must-do; 3 is the headline scale-up; 4–6 are the
tissue-diversity expansion.

## Non-goals for v0.4.x

- Cistarget kernel parity refresh against current ctxcore (separate
  follow-up; tracked on its own).
- AUCell wall-time / per-cell Pearson refresh against current pyscenic
  (separate follow-up; tracked on its own).
- Raw-10x `pipeline.run` without caller-side ATAC pre-subset (deferred
  to v0.5; documented workflow caveat).
- GPU execution (out of scope).
- Windows support (out of scope).

## What this plan does **not** claim

This is a *release-quality validation sweep*, not a methods-paper
benchmark. It does not claim rustscenic is "broadly validated" until
the gate above is cleared. Headline language stays as "validated on N
public multiome datasets" with N tracked exactly.
